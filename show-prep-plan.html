Assessment photos — how to shoot them

Light from the front or above (window / overhead), never behind — backlight kills all detail. Plain wall, phone chest-height, full body, ~6–8 ft back. Flexed and relaxed of each. These double as your first posing practice.

Back priority — confirms the one open weak point
  1. Rear double biceps. Back to camera, both arms curled up, elbows ~shoulder height. Flare the lats like spreading wings, squeeze the whole back. The key shot — width, thickness, rear delts, hams.
  2. Rear lat spread. Thumbs in your waistband at the lower back, drive elbows forward, flare lats as wide as possible. Shows your V.
  3. Back relaxed. Stand naturally, back to camera, arms down. Resting width + any L/R imbalance.
Legs confirm your strong suit
  1. Side quad. Turn side-on, front leg slightly forward and flexed, rise onto the ball of that foot to pop the quad sweep + hamstring.
  2. Front quad. Face camera, feet ~shoulder width, flex both quads hard with a slight lean back. Sweep + teardrop + separation.
  3. Copy the look: search YouTube for "NPC Classic Physique rear double biceps" and "side quad pose classic physique" (Terrence Ruffin has clean Classic breakdowns).
