Instagram & TikTok Posting Plan

The companion to the Facebook & Instagram plan — how to run the two video feeds that grow the audience and turn it into bookings.

The rule: TikTok is the engine (the real audience — ~12–14K), Instagram is the storefront (where a guest checks "is this place legit?" before booking). Same clips, different job. ~75% cute-animal reach · ~25% "you can stay here." People follow for the alpacas; they book because the experience looked real.

1 in 4 videos ends on a booking beat pointed at what the link can actually sell today — the Alpaca Walk ($50) — or "comment WAITLIST" for the glamping tents. Never imply the tents are bookable yet (permitting).

2 · How to run each feed

TikTok · @tapestry.acres
The growth engine

  • 4–7 posts / week. TikTok rewards volume + consistency far more than IG — this gets the most time.
  • Lo-fi beats polished. Ads-that-look-like-ads flop; wrap every promo inside an animal moment.
  • Cute hook in the first second — the scroll-stopper. Trending sounds on cute clips.
  • Pin 2–3 videos to the top: one "book an alpaca walk," one "glamping coming soon."
  • Reply with video — free reach, and where questions become bookings.
  • Geo-caption the drive-to market: "2 hrs from Nashville," "near Dale Hollow Lake."

Instagram · @tapestryacres
The booking storefront

  • 3–5 Reels / week (repurposed from TikTok) + Stories daily.
  • Watermark-free. Export from CapCut / the originals — never a TikTok download. IG down-ranks watermarked video.
  • Re-caption native. Drop #fyp (does nothing on IG); use the IG tag set below.
  • Build Story Highlights (0 today): Stay · Book Now · Glamping (soon) · Animals · Reviews · Events. Highest-ROI hour on IG.
  • Carousels for the stay experience — they earn the most saves.
  • Always geo-tag Monroe, TN.

Film once, post both — but re-caption per platform and strip the watermark for IG. One farm walk = 10+ clips = a week of content on both feeds.

4 · Recurring series (the cheapest content there is)

  • "Wake up at Tapestry Acres" — sunrise + animals. The stay pillar in one repeatable format.
  • "Meet the Herd Monday" — one named animal a week (rotate the whole herd, never run out).
  • A single alpaca's POV — ongoing character the audience follows.
  • "Building our glamping site" — document the tent build with a WAITLIST call-to-action. Turns the permitting wait into content and banks demand.

7 · Turn views into bookings (do these first)

The funnel fixes

  • One tracked link in both bios (Linktree / Stan + UTM) → the Alpaca Walk ($50) booking — the best actually-live product. Switch to cabins, then tents, as each becomes bookable.
  • Stand up a tent waitlist (free Wix form) and link it — bank the demand now.
  • Build the IG Story Highlights shelf — Stay, Book Now, Glamping (soon), Animals, Reviews, Events.
  • Rewrite both bios — value prop + location + CTA, each pointing at the other ("find us @tapestryacres on IG / @tapestry.acres on TikTok").

Keep the engine fed

  • Batch-film. One walk = a week of both feeds.
  • Post consistently — the current gap is cadence: don't let the feed go quiet for 2+ weeks.
  • Ask every guest for a Google review — cheapest, best-converting content (4.3★ → 4.5★ / 25+).
  • Reply fast, reply with video on TikTok for free reach.
