Key facts & gotchas

📐

Roof slope is built-in

Post A (120") vs Post B (108") = a 12" drop over 48" depth (~25% grade). The rear is the low, draining side — grade the ground away from it.

🚪

Doors swing outward

1/8" reveal all around. If they bind in humidity, plane the hinge side only — never the latch side (adjust hinges instead). Handles at ~42" from floor.

🪵

Prime every cut end

Freeze-dry plywood edges soak water. Sand 80-grit, 2 coats primer on raw edges, then 2 exterior-latex finish coats (120-grit between). Fascia edges & door tops are the weak points.

🏗️

Two-person roof lift

The rafter panel is built flat on the ground, then lifted onto the posts as one unit — projects 12" past the front. Don't try it solo.

Build sequence — 13 steps

1

Post & beam frame

Stand 4×4 Post A (120") and Post B (108") in front/back pairs. Attach 4×4 Beam C (96") across each pair's top — Posts B are shorter to create the roof slope. Brace diagonally with a temporary 2×4 to keep it square while fastening.

3" screws · 3 per jointpre-drillcheck square
2

Bottom support frame (joists)

Assemble the floor frame: Joist A (89") front-to-back, Joist B (41") side-to-side. Install 4 Brace Support C at the inside corners (both ends cut 45°) for racking resistance. Use joist hangers at the beam connections.

2½" screws45° brace cutsverify diagonal = square
3

Middle shelf support frame

Connect Shelf Frame A (38") cross-members to Shelf Frame B (96") rails. Set the lower cross-member 18½" off the floor, the upper at 37". Toe-screw to posts at 45°.

2½" screwslower @ 18½"upper @ 37"
4

Top shelf support frame

Frame with Top Shelf Frame A (19¼") cross-members + Top Shelf Frame B (96") rails. Add Top Shelf Brace C (29⅜", 45° both ends) diagonally — one per end — to stop racking. Keep the sloped-roof geometry.

2½" screwsbrace sits flush
5

Bottom shelf panel

Cut Bottom Shelf Panel A (96"×48"×¾") with 3½"×3½" notches at all 4 corners to clear the posts (offset 10-11/16" horizontal, 9-3/8" vertical). Jigsaw the inside corners. Drop onto the joist frame; secure with 1⅝" screws every 8".

1⅝" screws @ 8" o/cjigsaw notchestest-fit first
6

Middle shelf panel

Cut Middle Shelf Panel B (96"×41"×¾") — no notching; it sits between the posts at the 18½" position. Bead exterior glue on the rails, then fasten 1⅝" screws every 8".

1⅝" screws @ 8" o/cglue on rails
7

Top shelf panel

Cut Top Shelf Panel C (96"×22¼"×¾"), install on the upper frame against the rear. Front edge aligns to the inner face of the Top Shelf Frame A cross-members — check from the front before final fastening.

1⅝" screws @ 8" o/calign front edge
8

Roof rafter assembly

Build the rafter panel flat: 2 Roof Face Board B (120") as front/rear fascias, then 7 Roof Rafter A + 2 Roof Rafter B (85½") between them, spaced 16-15/16" c/c (Rafter B at the far edges). Lift onto the posts — roof projects 12" beyond the front. 2-person lift.

2½" assembly · 3" to posts16-15/16" c/c2-person lift
9

Wall siding — L / R / back

Start from the bottom. Side Panels A (48"×5") horizontal, 2 screws per post, 1/8" reveal gap between boards for drainage. Angle-cut the top pieces (Panel C) to the roof pitch. Back wall uses full-width Back Siding Panel A (97½").

1⅝" screws1/8" gap between boardsmeasure pitch before Panel C
10

Inner trim

Install Inner Trim A (89") horizontally as the inside header over the door opening, then 2× Inner Trim B (93¾") vertically as inside jambs. Flush to the inside framing face — this is the hinge-backing surface.

2½" screws · 3 per studflush to inside
11

Face trim (door surround)

Door Trims A (105") vertical on both exterior sides of the opening, flush to the siding front; Door Trim B (90½") horizontal across the top with 45° mitered corners. Overlap the raw panel edges, fill holes, caulk seams before painting.

2½" screws45° miter cornerscaulk seams
12

Metal roofing sheets

Install 3× 4'×8' corrugated galvanized sheets. Start from the low (rear) side, work to the high front, overlapping one full corrugation (~2"). 1½" self-tapping hex-head screws with EPDM washers through the crown, 2 per rafter crossing — compress the washer, don't crush it. Overhang the front fascia 1½"+ for a drip edge.

1½" roofing screwsstart low sidedon't over-tighten
13

Door assembly & hanging (×2)

Build each door flat: 2× Door Frame A (90-11/16") stiles + 2× Door Frame B (42-15/16") rails, 3 screws per corner. Face with 2× Door Panel A (4-5/8") at the edges + 6× Door Panel B (6") between (bottom gap 6¼", side setback 2-5/16", top gap 1-9/16"). Hang with 4 heavy hinges per door on the inner jambs, 1/8" reveal all around; handles at ~42"; barrel bolt / hasp at the meeting edge. Doors swing outward.

2½" frame · 1⅝" panels4 hinges/doorshim for even gap
