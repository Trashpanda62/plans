How the scores work

Every number is 0-100, higher is better, graded against the other family SUVs in this guide - not the whole car market. A 78 here means "good for a family SUV," not 78%.

The bands: 90-100 best-in-class 80-89 excellent 70-79 good 60-69 average under 60 a weak point. The bar colors match: green is strong, orange/red is a relative weakness.

The big Overall badge is a weighted blend of the eight scores, tilted toward what matters most to a parent: reliability & comfort 22% each, cargo & seating 12% each, safety 10%, mpg 8%, value 8%, cupholders 6%.

Scores are our editorial judgement, blending published specs (cargo, seats, mpg) with reliability and review consensus (Consumer Reports, iSeeCars, JD Power). They are a shopping shortcut, not gospel - always test-drive.
